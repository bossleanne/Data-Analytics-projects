Singaproe Primary School Workbook programs

Many people are studying in Singapore primary school these days, but want to apply for best primary school in Singapore, what kinds of Singapore primary school workbook do they need to prepare? I have summarized the four major workbook in Singapore primary school, all of which are excellent practice skiils to improve your result. Win a gold medal and you are almost one foot in the Oxbridge; win an invitation to the national team and you are almost one foot in the Ivy League.
Beside that, the famous workbook competitions for Singapore primary schools are: SMO, SChO, SPhO, and NOI, which involve four disciplines: Math, chemistry, physics, and informatics, we introduces you to each of them below to help you find the right Singapore primary school workbook competition for you!

1. SMO（Singapore Mathematical Olympiad

SMO is the largest primary school mathematics workbook competition in Singapore with thousands of participants each year.

How to register?


Registration is usually through the Singapore primary school, but you can also register yourself. There are three divisions, Singapore primary school workbook entry level, Singapore primary school workbook Senior level and Singapore primary school workbook intermediate level.
  • The age requirement for Singapore primary school workbook entry level is 9 years and under.
  • Singapore primary school workbook Senior level is under 12 years of age.
  • There is no age limit for the Open Group.
  • Specific age requirements will also be communicated for Singapore primary school workbook intermediate level

    • Awards

    Gold prizes will be awarded for answering 3/5 questions or more correctly in Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 2 and Silver prizes for the following; Bronze prizes will be awarded for those who do not make it to Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 2 but Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 1 is closer to the score line. In addition, the top 30 individual prizes in each Singapore primary school workbook competition category will be awarded by the organizers. The details fluctuate slightly each year depending on the difficulty of the topic.
    The SMO medals are an indicator of the Singapore primary school students in DSA and university applications, and are highly valued in Singapore.

    Competition Process

    Each Singapore primary school workbook division is divided into Round 1 and Round 2, and Round 1 winners can enter Round 2. Round 1 is usually two hours and 30 minutes long with 25 questions, while Round 2 requires you to answer five questions in four hours.

    In Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 1, Singapore primary school workbook Senior level has 25 multiple-choice questions and Open has 25 fill-in-the-blank questions. Commonly tested topics include polynomials, inequalities, number sequences, extreme values, number theory, arranging and combining, probability theory, trigonometry, triangles, and circles. Generally, a correct answer to Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 1 and an open answer to Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 2 are recommended.

    Content of the competition

    In Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 2, Singapore primary school workbook Senior level and Singapore primary school workbook entry level's questions are very similar, but the difficulty level is different, so we will introduce Singapore primary school workbook entry level questions as a reference.
    Geometry questions are usually about plane geometry, triangles and circles are still popular, and most of them can be solved simply by making auxiliary lines and basic properties of circles. Number theory questions are usually divisibility and congruence problems, which may require the use of properties such as Fermat's Small Theorem. Function problems usually use special points of functions (such as zero, symmetry points) and their basic properties such as factors. Dyeing problems have become more popular in recent years and are more obvious but difficult to prove, requiring clever solutions. As each question takes a long time, it is recommended that you choose to try the more confident questions first, and then study other topics. Even if you don't know how to do it, you may want to write down your ideas, and there may be step-by-step instructions. 3 correct answers to Round 2 will win the Gold Prize, and 3 or less will win the Silver Prize, depending on the difficulty of the question.

    Contest Time

    All Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 1 is at the beginning of the holiday season in June and Singapore primary school workbook competition Round 2 at the beginning of July, with little variation in exam times from year to year.

    2. SChO (Singapore Chemistry Olympiad).

    Singapore primary school workbook Chemistry Olympiad


    The Singapore primary school workbook Chemistry Olympiad has been held since 1989 by the Singapore National Institute of Chemistry (SNIC), the Chemistry Department of National University of Singapore. It consists of a theory exam and a lab exam.

    Competition Time

  • The Singapore primary school workbook Chemistry exam is on October 19, from 9 am to 12 noon.
  • The Singapore primary school workbook Chemistry lab exam is on Nov. 30, 9 a.m. to 12 noon.

    • Content of the competition

    Similar to the basic syllabus of the IChO (International Chemistry Olympiad) , the type of questions is Long-structured questions, which are not fixed and may vary depending on the year.

    Awards

    In general, around 20 gold medals, 40 to 50 silver medals and some bronze medals will be awarded to the best overall performance of the students, and the best student award will also be given out. The results are usually presented at the beginning of the following year. As the number of medals is small, a medal is very valuable and will be very helpful to the application.

    3. PhO (Singapore Physics Olympiad).

    Singapore primary school workbook Physics Olympiad


    How to register
    Singapore primary school workbook physics registration is uniformly declared through the school, Singapore primary school workbook physics official does not accept individual registration

    competition time

    Singapore primary school workbook physics theoretical round will be held on or around October 20. The Singapore primary school workbook physics second experimental round will be held on or around November 20.

    Competition Flow

    In the Singapore primary school workbook physics theory exam, there will be two papers Paper 1 for 2 hours before lunch and Paper 2 also for 2 hours after lunch. About 50 students who performed well in the Singapore primary school workbook physics first round will be selected for the Singapore primary school workbook physics second round of the competition, and the teachers will be informed first.

    Exam Preparation Resources

    Previous years Singapore primary school workbook physics questions and answers can be found on the official SPhO website, while SPhO questions and answers will not be released.

    4.National Olympiad in Informatics (NOI).

    Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics Competition

    Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics Competition is a nationwide large-scale programming competition in Singapore and is open to all primary school students. Students can use C++, JAVA or Python. Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics Competition winners will also be selected to undergo further training with the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI) training team. They will then be able to compete for a place on the prestigious Singapore IOI team for the International Olympiad in Informatics to be held in Baku, Azerbaijan from 4 August to 11 August 2019.

    How to register

    For each Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics Competition category, each Singapore primary school may send up to five students. Deadline for entries: 1st March 2019.

    competition content

    The formal Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics competition requires 3-5 tasks in C++, Java and Python within 5 hours. All the tasks in the Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics Competition are algorithm based and the organizing committee requires students to design correct and easy to use algorithms to solve real world problems.

    Duration of the competition

    The official Singapore primary school workbook National Informatics competition will be on March 23, 2019. New for the start of 2019 is the addition of a preliminaries section on March 22, 2019, where participants are required to complete 1 task in 3 hours.
